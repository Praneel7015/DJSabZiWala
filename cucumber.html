Cucumber Vegetable History

Cucumber (Cucumis sativus) is a vine that originated in South Asia, but now grows on most continents. It's high in water content and low in calories, fat, cholesterol, and sodium. It's usually sold fresh, but also processed into pickles.

Known for their crisp texture and refreshing taste, cucumbers are consumed in a variety of ways, including raw in salads, pickled, or used in cooking. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Cucumbers are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate regions. The cultivation of different cucumber varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
