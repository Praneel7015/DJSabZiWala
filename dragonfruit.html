Dragon Fruit

This tropical fruit is known for its vibrant red skin and sweet, seed-speckled pulp. It tastes like a cross between a pear and a kiwi. You can slice and eat the fruit as-is, try it with yogurt, or add it to a smoothie or salad.

Dragon Fruit History

Dragon fruit, also known as pitaya or strawberry pear, grows on the Hylocereus cactus, also known as the Honolulu queen, whose flowers only open at night. The plant is native to southern Mexico and Central America. Today, it is grown all over the world.

Known for their versatility, dragon fruits are consumed fresh, used in cooking, and processed into various products. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Dragon fruits are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in tropical regions. The cultivation of different dragon fruit varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and textures.
