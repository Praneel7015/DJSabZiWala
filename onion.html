Onion Vegetable History

Onion (Allium cepa) is a bulbous vegetable that originated in Asia. It's high in vitamins C and B6, and is known for its unique pungent flavor and aroma. It's usually sold fresh, but also processed into products like onion powder and onion oil.

Known for their versatility, onions are consumed in a variety of ways, including raw in salads, caramelized, or cooked in soups and stews. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Onions are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate regions. The cultivation of different onion varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
