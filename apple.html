Apple Fruit History

Apples, scientifically known as Malus domestica, belong to the Rosaceae family. These crisp and sweet fruits have a long and diverse history.

Apples have been cultivated for thousands of years in Asia and Europe and were brought to North America by European colonists. They have become one of the most popular fruits globally.

Known for their versatility, apples are consumed fresh, used in various culinary applications, and processed into products like apple juice and applesauce. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Apples are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate regions with distinct seasons. The cultivation of different apple varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and textures.
