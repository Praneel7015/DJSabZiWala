Lemon Fruit History

Lemons, scientifically known as Citrus × limon, belong to the Rutaceae family. These fruits have a long and diverse history.

Lemons are native to Asia, primarily Northeast India, Northern Myanmar, and China. The tree's ellipsoidal yellow fruit is used for culinary and non-culinary purposes throughout the world, primarily for its juice, which has both culinary and cleaning uses. The pulp and rind are also used in cooking and baking.

Known for their versatility, lemons are consumed fresh, used in cooking, and processed into various products. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Lemons are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate regions with distinct seasons. The cultivation of different lemon varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and textures.
