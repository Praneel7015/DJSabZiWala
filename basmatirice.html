Basmati Rice History

Basmati rice is a variety of long, slender-grained aromatic rice which is traditionally grown in India and Pakistan. Among the types of rice known, Basmati has the longest grains and is known for its distinctive aroma and flavour.

Known for its unique aroma and high nutritional value, basmati rice is consumed in a variety of ways, including in biryanis, pulao, or as a side dish. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Basmati rice is grown in a variety of climates but thrive in the Indian subcontinent. The cultivation of different basmati rice varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
