Chickpea Pulse History

Chickpeas (Cicer arietinum) are a type of legume in the family Fabaceae, subfamily Faboideae. Its different types are variously known as gram or Bengal gram, garbanzo beans, Egyptian peas. Chickpea seeds are high in protein.

Known for their versatility and high nutritional value, chickpeas are consumed in a variety of ways, including in salads, soups and stews, or as a key ingredient in many dishes like hummus and chana masala. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Chickpeas are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate and subtropical regions. The cultivation of different chickpea varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
