Walnut Nut History

Walnuts (Juglans regia) are a type of nut native to the region stretching from the Balkan Peninsula eastward to the Himalayas and southwest China. They're high in fiber, protein, and various essential nutrients. They're usually sold shelled and raw or roasted.

Known for their rich flavor and crunchy texture, walnuts are consumed in a variety of ways, including raw, roasted, or as a key ingredient in many dishes like walnut bread and walnut salad. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Walnut trees are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate regions. The cultivation of different walnut varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
