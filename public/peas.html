Pea Vegetable History

Peas (Pisum sativum) are a type of legume that originated in the Mediterranean basin. They're high in fiber, protein, vitamins A, B6, C, and K, and several minerals. They're usually sold fresh, canned, or frozen.

Known for their sweet taste and starchy texture, peas are consumed in a variety of ways, including boiled, steamed, or raw in salads. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Peas are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in cooler regions. The cultivation of different pea varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
