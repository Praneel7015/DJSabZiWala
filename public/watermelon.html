Watermelon Fruit History

Watermelons, scientifically known as Citrullus lanatus, belong to the Cucurbitaceae family[^1^][1]. These fruits have a long and diverse history[^1^][1].

Watermelons are native to tropical Africa and are grown in tropical and subtropical areas worldwide[^1^][1]. Watermelon fruits are processed into jams, jellies, and preserves and are common pastry fillings[^1^][1].

Known for their versatility, watermelons are consumed fresh, used in cooking, and processed into various products[^1^][1]. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories[^1^][1].

Watermelons are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in tropical regions[^1^][1]. The cultivation of different watermelon varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and textures[^1^][1].
