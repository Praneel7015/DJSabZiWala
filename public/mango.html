Mango Fruit History

Mangoes, scientifically known as Mangifera indica, belong to the Anacardiaceae family. These fruits have a long and diverse history. Mangoes are native to southern Asia, particularly in eastern India, Bangladesh, and the Andaman Islands. Mangoes are a rich source of vitamins A, C, and D.

Known for their versatility, mangoes are consumed fresh, used in cooking, and processed into various products. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Mangoes are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in tropical regions with distinct seasons. The cultivation of different mango varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and textures.
