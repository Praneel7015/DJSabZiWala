Broccoli Vegetable History

Broccoli (Brassica oleracea var. italica) is a cruciferous vegetable that originated in Italy. It's high in many nutrients, including fiber, vitamin C, vitamin K, iron, and potassium. It's usually sold fresh, but also available frozen.

Known for their dense green clusters of flower buds, broccoli is consumed in a variety of ways, including boiled, steamed, stir-fried, or raw in salads. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Broccoli is grown in a variety of climates but thrive in cooler regions. The cultivation of different broccoli varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
