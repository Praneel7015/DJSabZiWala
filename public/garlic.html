Garlic Vegetable History

Garlic (Allium sativum) is a species in the onion genus, Allium. It's native to Central Asia and northeastern Iran, and has long been a common seasoning worldwide. It's usually sold fresh, but also processed into products like garlic powder and garlic oil.

Known for its strong aroma and flavor, garlic is consumed in a variety of ways, including raw in salads, used in cooking, or used as a spice. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Garlic is grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate regions. The cultivation of different garlic varieties has led to a wide range of flavors and sizes.
