Black Bean Pulse History

Black beans (Phaseolus vulgaris) are a variety of the common bean that originated in the Americas. They're high in protein, fiber, and various essential nutrients. They're usually sold dried, but also available canned.

Known for their earthy flavor and creamy texture, black beans are consumed in a variety of ways, including in soups, stews, salads, or as a key ingredient in many dishes like black bean soup and burritos. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Black beans are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate and subtropical regions. The cultivation of different black bean varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
