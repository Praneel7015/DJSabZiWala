Lentil Pulse History

Lentils (Lens culinaris) are a type of legume that originated in Near East and Mediterranean area. They're high in fiber, protein, and various essential nutrients. They're usually sold dried, but also available canned.

Known for their earthy flavor and high nutritional value, lentils are consumed in a variety of ways, including in soups, stews, salads, or as a key ingredient in many dishes like dal. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Lentils are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate and subtropical regions. The cultivation of different lentil varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
