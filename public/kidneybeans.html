Kidney Bean Pulse History

Kidney beans (Phaseolus vulgaris) are a variety of the common bean that originated in Central and South America. They're high in protein, fiber, and various essential nutrients. They're usually sold dried, but also available canned.

Known for their robust flavor and soft texture, kidney beans are consumed in a variety of ways, including in soups, stews, salads, or as a key ingredient in many dishes like chili con carne and rajma. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Kidney beans are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate and subtropical regions. The cultivation of different kidney bean varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
