Mint Vegetable History

Mint (Mentha) is a perennial herb that originated in the Mediterranean region. It's known for its aromatic and refreshing flavor. It's usually sold fresh, but also processed into products like mint oil and dried mint leaves.

Known for their cooling sensation, mint leaves are consumed in a variety of ways, including in teas, beverages, salads, and used as a garnish. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Mint plants are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate regions. The cultivation of different mint varieties has led to a wide range of flavors and aromas.
