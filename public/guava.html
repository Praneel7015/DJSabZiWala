Guava Fruit History

Guavas, scientifically known as Psidium guajava, belong to the Myrtaceae family[^1^][1]. These fruits have a long and diverse history[^1^][1].

Guavas are native to tropical America and are grown in tropical and subtropical areas worldwide[^2^][2]. Guava fruits are processed into jams, jellies, and preserves and are common pastry fillings[^2^][2].

Known for their versatility, guavas are consumed fresh, used in cooking, and processed into various products[^1^][1]. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories[^1^][1].

Guavas are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in tropical regions[^1^][1]. The cultivation of different guava varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and textures[^1^][1].
