Carrot Vegetable History

Carrots, scientifically known as Daucus carota, belong to the Apiaceae family. These vegetables have a long and diverse history. Carrots are native to Europe and southwestern Asia and have been cultivated for centuries for their sweet and nutritious roots.

Known for their versatility, carrots are consumed fresh, used in cooking, and processed into various products. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Carrots are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate regions with distinct seasons. The cultivation of different carrot varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and textures.
