Orange Fruit History

Oranges, scientifically known as Citrus sinensis, belong to the Rutaceae family. These fruits have a long and diverse history.

Oranges are native to China and are grown in many areas around the world, including the United States, Mexico, and Spain. Oranges are a treasure trove of nutrients and protective plant compounds, including vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. Studies show that consuming oranges regularly may benefit your health in several ways.

Known for their versatility, oranges are consumed fresh, used in cooking, and processed into various products. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Oranges are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in temperate regions with distinct seasons. The cultivation of different orange varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and textures.
