Cashew Nut History

Cashews (Anacardium occidentale) are a type of nut native to tropical regions of Brazil. They're high in fiber, protein, and various essential nutrients. They're usually sold roasted, but also available raw.

Known for their rich flavor and creamy texture, cashews are consumed in a variety of ways, including raw, roasted, or as a key ingredient in many dishes like cashew chicken and vegan cheese. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Cashew trees are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in tropical regions. The cultivation of different cashew varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
