Cabbage Vegetable History

Cabbage (Brassica oleracea) is a leafy green vegetable that originated in Europe. It's high in vitamins C and K and is known for its high fiber content. It's usually sold fresh, but also processed into products like sauerkraut and coleslaw.

Known for their versatility, cabbages are consumed in a variety of ways, including boiled, steamed, sautéed, or raw in salads. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Cabbages are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in cooler regions. The cultivation of different cabbage varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and textures.
