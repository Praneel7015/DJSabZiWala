Pecan Nut History

Pecans (Carya illinoinensis) are a type of nut native to the southern United States and Mexico. They're high in fiber, protein, and various essential nutrients. They're usually sold shelled and raw or roasted.

Known for their rich flavor and buttery texture, pecans are consumed in a variety of ways, including raw, roasted, or as a key ingredient in many dishes like pecan pie and praline candy. They also hold cultural significance in many traditions and stories.

Pecan trees are grown in a variety of climates but thrive in subtropical regions. The cultivation of different pecan varieties has led to a wide range of flavors, colors, and sizes.
